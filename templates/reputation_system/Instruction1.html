The experiment has multiple rounds. In the experiment, participants will be equally divided into two roles: Player A and Player B. Your role does not change during the experiment. Participants are divided into groups of 4 people with 2 player A and 2 player B. You will be grouped with the same participants during the experiment.

Each round has multiple stages as follows:
Stage 1: All player B choose between O and E. If player B chooses O, her payoff is determined and her round ends. If player B chooses E, she will move to Stage 2.
Stage 2: If the experiment allows, player B who choose E at Stage 1 can choose the price of her E. Otherwise price will be given by the system and Stage 2 will be skipped.
Stage 3: Observing player Bs' past choices and the price of E in the current round, Player A chooses which player B to interact with by choosing the corresponding ID of player B. Player A can only interact with player B who chooses E at Stage 1. Player A can also choose to interact with no one to guarantee some payoff by choosing 0.
Stage 4: If player B has been interacted with one or more player A, she chooses either X or Y, which determined the payoff of the players she is interacting with.

The results will be calculated after stage 4. All participants receive the payoff in the current round and the experiment moves to the next round.
